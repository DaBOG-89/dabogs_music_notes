Keyboard Instruments

Keyboard instruments are musical instruments that are played using a keyboard — a set of keys that the performer presses with their fingers to produce sound. The keyboard allows the player to control pitch, harmony, and sometimes dynamics.

Main types of keyboard instruments:

1. String-based (older forms)

  • Harpsichord – Strings are plucked when keys are pressed.
  • Clavichord – Strings are struck by small metal tangents.
  • Piano – Strings are struck by felt hammers when keys are pressed; allows soft and loud playing depending on touch (hence its name pianoforte).

2. Wind-based (with pipes)

Pipe Organ – Keys control air flow through pipes of different lengths, producing sound.

3. Electronic keyboards

  • Synthesizers – Generate sound electronically, imitating many instruments.
  • Digital pianos / Keyboards – Electronic versions of pianos or other sounds.

Key features:

  • Keyboard layout: usually white and black keys in a repeating pattern.
  • Versatility: can play melody and harmony at the same time.
  • Used in classical, jazz, pop, gospel, electronic, and almost every style of music.
