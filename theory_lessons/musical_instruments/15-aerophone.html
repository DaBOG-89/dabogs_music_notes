Aerophone Instruments

An aerophone is a type of musical instrument that produces sound mainly by causing a column of air to vibrate. Unlike chordophones (strings), membranophones (skins), or idiophones (self-sounding), aerophones rely on air as the primary vibrating medium.

Western instruments:

  • Flute, piccolo (air blown across a hole)
  • Trumpet, trombone, tuba (lip vibration in a mouthpiece)
  • Clarinet, saxophone, oboe, bassoon (reed instruments)
  • Pipe organ, harmonica, accordion (free reed aerophones)

African traditional aerophones:

  • Algaita (Hausa double-reed instrument, similar to oboe)
  • Kakaki (long metal trumpet, used in Hausa royal ceremonies)
  • Fulani flute (tambin)
  • Oja (Igbo wooden flute)
