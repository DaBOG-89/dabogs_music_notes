The Talking Drum

A talking drum is a West African drum that can mimic the tones and rhythms of human speech. It has an hourglass shape with strings (tension cords) running between its two drumheads.

The drum is played with a curved stick or the hand. When the player squeezes the cords under the arm while striking the drum, the pitch changes, allowing the drum to “talk” — imitating the tone, rhythm, and pitch of spoken language.

Hourglass-shaped body. Two drumheads connected by leather tension cords. Played with a curved stick or hand. Can change pitch by squeezing the cords.

The talking drum is used in traditional African music, ceremonies, storytelling, and communication between villages. It was historically used to send messages before modern communication existed. In Nigeria, the talking drum is known by different names — for example, “Dùndún” among the Yoruba people.

A talking drum player is called a talking drummer or sometimes a master drummer.
