Orchestra Classification

In an orchestra, instruments are grouped into four main families. This is called the orchestral classification of musical instruments:

1. Strings

  • Usually the largest section of the orchestra.
  • Played with a bow or plucked.
  • Examples: Violin, Viola, Cello, Double Bass, Harp.

2. Woodwinds

  • Sound produced by blowing air into the instrument (through a reed or across a hole).
  • Examples: Flute, Oboe, Clarinet, Bassoon, Piccolo.

3. Brass

  • Sound produced by buzzing lips into a mouthpiece.
  • Loud, powerful, and often used for fanfares or strong passages.
  • Examples: Trumpet, Trombone, French Horn, Tuba.

4. Percussion

  • Played by striking, shaking, or scraping.
  • Can provide rhythm, accents, or even melody (if pitched).
  • Examples: Timpani, Snare Drum, Bass Drum, Cymbals, Triangle, Xylophone, Glockenspiel.
