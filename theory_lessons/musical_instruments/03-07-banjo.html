The Banjo

A banjo is a stringed musical instrument with a round body, a long neck, and metal strings. It has a bright, lively sound that’s often used in folk, country, bluegrass, and African-American traditional music.

The body (or pot) of a banjo looks like a drum, with a thin plastic or animal-skin membrane stretched over a circular frame. It usually has 4, 5, or 6 strings, depending on the style of music. The strings are stretched over a long neck with frets, similar to a guitar. It’s played by plucking or strumming the strings with the fingers or a pick.

Types of Banjos

  • 5-string banjo – The most common; used in bluegrass and country.
  • 4-string banjo – Used in jazz and Irish folk music.
  • 6-string banjo – Tuned like a guitar, for easier transition for guitar players.

The banjo’s sound is bright, sharp, and rhythmic, giving songs a cheerful, bouncy feel. In bluegrass or country music, it often plays fast, rolling patterns called “banjo rolls.” It’s also used in folk, jazz, African, and Caribbean music traditions.

A banjo player is called a banjoist.
