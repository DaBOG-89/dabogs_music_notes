Brass Instruments

Brass instruments are a family of musical instruments that produce sound when the player buzzes their lips into a cup-shaped mouthpiece. The vibration of the lips creates sound waves that travel through the tubing of the instrument, and the pitch is changed by pressing valves or moving a slide, as well as by tightening or loosening the lips. They are usually made of brass (a metal alloy of copper and zinc), though the defining feature is how they produce sound, not the material.

Examples of brass instruments:

  • Trumpet
  • Trombone (uses a slide instead of valves)
  • French horn
  • Tuba
  • Cornet
  • Euphonium

Key features:

  • Made of long metal tubing, coiled into a compact shape.
  • Have a flared bell at the end.
  • Valves or slides adjust the length of tubing to change notes.
  • Produce a bright, powerful, and often bold sound, common in orchestras, bands, and jazz.
