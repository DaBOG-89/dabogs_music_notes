String Instruments

String instruments are musical instruments that produce sound by vibrating strings. The vibration of the strings can be created in different ways — by plucking, striking, or bowing them. The body of the instrument (often made of wood or other resonant material) helps amplify the sound.

Types of string instruments

  • Plucked instruments – The strings are plucked with fingers or a pick. Examples: guitar, harp, banjo, kora.
  • Bowed instruments – The strings are played with a bow (a stick strung with horsehair). Examples: violin, viola, cello, double bass.
  • Struck instruments – The strings are struck by hammers or similar mechanisms. Examples: piano, hammered dulcimer.

Key Features

  • Pitch: Changed by opening or closing holes with fingers or keys.
  • Sound: Softer and more airy than brass instruments.
  • Materials: Traditionally wood, but modern versions (like saxophones) can be brass or other metals — they are still woodwinds because of the way they produce sound.
