The Tambourine

A tambourine is a small handheld percussion instrument that consists of a round wooden or plastic frame with small metal jingles (called zills) attached around it.

Some tambourines also have a drumhead stretched over one side, while others are headless (just the frame and jingles). The player shakes, strikes, or taps it with their hand or fingers to make rhythmic jingling sounds.

Round frame (wood or plastic). Has metal jingles (zills) around the edge. May have a drumhead or be headless. Played by shaking, striking, or tapping.

The tambourine is used in orchestras, bands, gospel music, folk music, and dance performances. It adds rhythm, sparkle, and accent to musical pieces.

A tambourine player is called a tambourinist or percussionist.
