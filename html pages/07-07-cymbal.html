The Cymbal

A cymbal is a percussion instrument made of thin, round metal plates that produce a crashing or shimmering sound when struck.

Cymbals are usually made of brass or bronze and can be played in pairs (clashed together) or individually with drumsticks or mallets. They come in different sizes, each creating a unique sound â€” from soft and shimmering to loud and explosive.

Made of metal (usually bronze or brass). Round, flat, and concave shape. Played by striking or clashing them. Produces bright, ringing, or crashing tones.

Cymbals are used in drum kits, orchestras, marching bands, and traditional music to add rhythm, accents, and dramatic effects. There are different types, such as crash cymbals, ride cymbals, and hi-hats.

A cymbal drum player is called a cymbalist or percussionist.
