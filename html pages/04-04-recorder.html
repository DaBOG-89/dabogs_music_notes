The Recorder

A recorder is a woodwind musical instrument that produces sound when you blow air into it and cover or uncover holes with your fingers to change the pitch.

The recorder is a simple flute-like instrument made of wood or plastic. It has a mouthpiece (called a fipple), a long body with finger holes, and a foot joint. Sound is made by blowing gently into the mouthpiece and changing finger positions to play different notes.
