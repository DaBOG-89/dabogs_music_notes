The Triangle

A triangle is a small metal percussion instrument shaped like a triangle, usually made from a steel rod that is bent into a three-sided form with one corner left open.

The triangle is suspended by a thin string or cord and played by striking it with a metal beater. It produces a bright, ringing, and shimmering sound that can cut through the texture of an orchestra or band.

Made of metal (usually steel). Triangular shape with one open corner. Played by hitting with a metal beater. Produces a clear, high-pitched ringing tone.

The triangle is used in orchestras, bands, folk music, and school music to add sparkle, rhythm, and accents. Despite its simple appearance, it plays an important role in adding brightness to the overall sound.

A triangle player is called a triangle player or percussionist.
