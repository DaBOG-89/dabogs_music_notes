The Saxophone

A saxophone is a woodwind instrument made of brass but played with a single reed like a clarinet. It produces a strong, smooth, and expressive sound that’s used in many styles of music.

The saxophone has a curved metal body with many keys that the player presses to change notes. It’s played by blowing into a mouthpiece fitted with a reed, which vibrates to make sound. The instrument’s wide, flaring bell helps project its tone.

Made of brass but belongs to the woodwind family because it uses a reed. Has a single-reed mouthpiece like the clarinet. Comes in different sizes — soprano, alto, tenor, and baritone are the most common. Known for its rich, smooth, and emotional tone.

The saxophone is used in jazz, blues, classical, rock, and military band music. It can play both melodies and solos, often standing out because of its expressive sound.

A saxophone player is called a saxophonist.
