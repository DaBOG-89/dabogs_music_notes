Percussion

Percussion instruments are musical instruments that produce sound when they are struck, shaken, or scraped. The sound comes from the material of the instrument itself vibrating, without the need for strings or air columns.

Two main types of percussion instruments:

  • Pitched percussion – produce definite notes (can play melodies).
    Examples: Xylophone, Marimba, Glockenspiel, Timpani.
  • Unpitched percussion – produce sounds without definite pitch (mainly rhythm).
    Examples: Drums (snare drum, bass drum), Cymbals, Tambourine, Triangle.

Key features:

  • They are often the rhythmic foundation of music.
  • Found in orchestras, bands, traditional/folk music, and modern genres.
  • Can be played with hands, sticks, mallets, or beaters.
