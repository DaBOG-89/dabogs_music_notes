The Xylophone

A xylophone is a percussion instrument made up of a series of wooden bars of different lengths that produce musical notes when struck with mallets. Each bar gives a different pitch, and the bars are arranged like a piano keyboard.

The xylophone’s bars are usually made of hardwood such as rosewood or padauk and are mounted over resonator tubes that amplify the sound. The player strikes the bars with rubber or wooden mallets, producing a bright, cheerful tone.

Made of wooden bars arranged in order of pitch. Played by striking the bars with mallets. Produces a bright, sharp, and ringing sound. Comes in various sizes for solo or orchestral use.

The xylophone is used in orchestras, bands, African traditional music, and school music education. It’s often used for melodies, rhythm patterns, and colorful effects in musical pieces.

A xylophone player is called a xylophonist.
