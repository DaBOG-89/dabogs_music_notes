The Cornet

A cornet is a brass wind instrument that looks and sounds similar to a trumpet, but it has a shorter and more compact shape and produces a softer, mellower tone.

The cornet is made of brass and has three valves like the trumpet. Its tubing is more conical (gradually widening), which gives it a warmer sound. Players produce notes by buzzing their lips into the mouthpiece and pressing the valves to change pitch.

Made of brass with three piston valves. Has a conical bore, unlike the trumpet’s cylindrical one. Produces a warm, mellow, and smooth tone. Commonly tuned in B♭.

The cornet is often used in brass bands, military bands, and sometimes in orchestras and jazz ensembles. It usually plays melodic parts and blends smoothly with other brass instruments.

A cornet player is called a cornetist or cornet player.
