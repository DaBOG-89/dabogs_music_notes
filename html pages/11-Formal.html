1. Idiophones

Instruments that produce sound from the vibration of their own body, without strings, membranes, or external air.
Examples: Xylophone, Cymbals, Triangle, Maracas, Gong, Castanets.

2. Membranophones

Instruments that produce sound through a stretched membrane (skin) that vibrates when struck, rubbed, or sung into.
Examples: Drums (snare drum, bass drum, talking drum, tabla, bongos, congas).

3. Chordophones

Instruments that produce sound from vibrating strings stretched between two points.
Examples: Violin, Guitar, Harp, Piano, Cello, Sitar, Kora.

4. Aerophones

Instruments that produce sound by vibrating air. Examples: Flute, Clarinet, Trumpet, Saxophone, Oboe, Pipe Organ, Horn.

5. Electrophones (added later)

Instruments that produce sound electronically or need electricity to amplify their sound. Examples: Synthesizer, Electric guitar, Drum machine, Digital keyboard, Theremin.
