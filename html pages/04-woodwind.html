Woodwind Instruments

Woodwind instruments are a family of musical instruments that produce sound when air is blown through them, causing a column of air inside to vibrate. They are called woodwinds because traditionally they were made of wood, though today many are made from metal, plastic, or a mix.

Types of Woodwind Instruments

1. Flutes (air is blown across an opening)

No reed. Examples: flute, piccolo, panpipes, recorder.

2. Single-reed instruments (air vibrates a single reed attached to a mouthpiece)

Examples: clarinet, saxophone.

3. Double-reed instruments (air vibrates two reeds tied together)

Examples: oboe, bassoon, English horn.

Key Features

  • Pitch: Changed by opening or closing holes with fingers or keys.
